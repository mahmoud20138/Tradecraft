# Lewis Borsellino's 10 Commandments of Trading

> **Source:** Lewis Borsellino — CME floor trader legend. Traded for Goldman Sachs, Merrill Lynch,
> EF Hutton. Made $4.7M in a single day (1987). At one point responsible for 10% of S&P's
> total volume. 20 years of P&L without a single losing year.
> Words of Rizdom podcast, Part 2 (March 2026, 71K views).

## Background: Who Is Lewis Borsellino?

- Started as an order filler in the Chicago Mercantile Exchange (CME)
- Rose to become the **largest individual S&P futures trader** in the pit
- Featured in USA Today as the biggest S&P futures trader in the country
- **20 consecutive profitable years** — maybe 10 losing months total across two decades
- Traded ~1,000 contracts daily, sometimes making 2-3 ticks, sometimes 100 points
- Founded TeachTrade.com in 1996 (20,000 subscribers from 19 countries)
- Built an algorithmic trading system (5-min candle open range) that made $2M in 6 months
- Later founded a cybersecurity company (3 employees to 100+)

## The 10 Commandments

### 1. Trade for Success, Not Money

> "If you're good at reading the market, the money follows."

- Focus on **process excellence**, not dollar amounts
- When you obsess over P&L, fear and hesitation creep in
- Many traders' careers end early because they fixate on how much they made/lost
- The competition is against the market and other traders, not a dollar target
- **Money is a byproduct** of following your trading plan correctly

### 2. Discipline Is Everything

> "If you can't isolate yourself, shut off your phone, and focus only on your trading decisions,
> you're going to have problems."

- Eliminate all distractions during trading hours
- Monday to Friday = 100% dedicated to the craft
- No alcohol, no drugs, no bad habits during trading days
- Many traders get stuck in losing positions because they **can't accept the loss**
- Enter each day with a **clean slate** — yesterday's results don't matter
- "What have you done for me today?" mentality

### 3. Know Yourself

> "Everyone has different levels of fear and greed."

- Understand your **personal risk tolerance** before sizing up
- Some traders are excellent at 1-2 lots making $300-500K/year with minimal risk
- The moment they size up to 5 lots, performance collapses — the money affects decisions
- **Don't let money influence your judgment** — know exactly where your comfort zone is
- You CAN grow from 1 lot to 100 lots — but it must be gradual and earned
- "You don't need to be a rocket scientist to trade"

**Key insight:** The people Lewis expected to succeed often failed. The "simpler" traders
who didn't overthink outperformed Harvard MBAs who tried to rationalize every market move.

### 4. Kill Your Ego

> "The markets will humble you. You think you're making money every day for a month,
> you feel like you can't do anything wrong. Then a catastrophic event hits."

- After a winning streak, you feel invincible — this is when you're most vulnerable
- Ego makes you take outsized risks, chasing "the big one"
- Worst scenario: a series of losses leads to **revenge trading** — risking everything
  to recover, instead of stepping back to reassess
- "I was always the first to laugh at myself. I'd tell people: I'm not as smart as you think."
- **Don't believe your own hype.** When people inflate your image, that's dangerous
- A small dose of ego helps you push boundaries, but overall ego is destructive

### 5. No Room for Hope, Wishing, or Praying

> "When you're in a trade and start bargaining with yourself, that's the problem."

- When the market moves against you: "It'll stop here" → "Maybe I should lighten up" →
  "No, let me add more" → **Averaging into a loser** → Catastrophic loss
- Every trade MUST have a **predetermined stop loss**
- Traders break all rules hoping to be right — then the "men in white coats" come
  to pull you from the pit because your account is empty
- **Accepting loss is the hardest thing a human can do** — but it's non-negotiable in trading
- "Imagine working all week and your boss says 'Great job, but I'm not paying you.'
  That's trading. Some weeks you make nothing."

### 6. Let Profits Run, Cut Losses Short

> "When I saw the algorithm was right and institutional buying was flooding in,
> I increased my position immediately."

- Lewis's algorithm: 600-point stop, 1,000-point target
- When order flow confirmed the direction, he **added to winners** (not losers)
- Base hits are good, but the **big PnL comes from sizing up on the best setups**
- Most of a trader's annual profit comes from a **handful of exceptional trades**
- Richard Dennis (Turtles) example: position trader who held for months on the best trends
- "The fastest way to turn a day trader into an investor is to let the market go against them"
  — because they refuse to cut the loss

### 7. Know When to Trade and When to Wait

> "Before unemployment numbers on Friday, trading Thursday is not a good idea.
> The market will be choppy."

- Don't trade in dead, low-volatility environments
- **Be where the action is** — volatility and order flow
- "The worst thing is being stuck in a market with no volatility and no liquidity"
- Pre-news days are typically choppy — the only winner from overtrading is the broker
- This applies TODAY: many forex traders insist on trading EUR during low-liquidity periods
  and wonder why they can't make money

### 8. Love Your Losers as Much as Your Winners

> "Any lesson you've ever learned in life — if it didn't cause you some pain,
> it wasn't really a lesson."

- **Losses are your real teacher.** Winners teach you nothing new.
- If you break rules and get away with it (overtrade and still profit, average down and recover),
  you'll keep breaking rules until the rules break YOU
- Lewis lost ~$50M at age 57-58 in a nursing home business — taught him never to personally
  guarantee all loans again
- "You have to learn from your losses in life, or luck won't save you"
- What did your losing trades teach you? That's the real question.

### 9. After Three Consecutive Losses, Take a Break

> "I put a circuit breaker on traders I was backing so they could clear their heads."

- Lewis funded traders and installed mandatory break rules after 3 losses
- Funded traders panic: "Lewis will fire me if I don't start making money"
- **Story of Brad Sullivan:** Lost $25K in 4 months. Lewis told him: "Don't worry about
  the money. Just focus on YOUR process." Brad made $1M by year end.
- The break isn't about the strategy being wrong — it's about **mental reset**
- "Focus on the process, not the money. Follow your rules. That's all you need to do."

### 10. The Unbreakable Rule: Never Break the Rules

> "You might break a rule and survive. But eventually, the rules will break you."

- Every commandment reinforces every other one
- Occasionally you break a rule and get lucky — this creates a dangerous false confidence
- If you **consistently violate** trading commandments, you'll pay with your profits
- The challenge: controlling ego when you've made $100K in a day or two
- Money is pure emotion — combine large sums with short timeframes and ego, and disaster follows

## Lewis's Core Principles (Beyond the 10)

### On Order Flow
- "I studied who was buying, who was selling, where the buy stops and sell stops were"
- Used 3 different analytical approaches simultaneously:
  1. **GANN analysis** specialist
  2. **Reversal point** specialist (tops and bottoms)
  3. **Day trading** specialist
- Combined all three to make real-time decisions

### On Athletes as Traders
- Actively recruited athletes for trading — they understand repetition and drill
- "In trading, there's no practice day. Every day is game day."
- Athletes train their bodies through repetition → traders train their reactions the same way
- Speed of reaction + pattern recognition = the edge

### On AI in Trading
- Former partner reduced data/signal costs from $200K/year to $5K with AI automation
- "AI will create more millionaires than you can imagine"
- BUT: concerned about AI bubble forming
- Warning: employees putting proprietary data into public LLMs = massive corporate risk

### On the Current Market
- Gold heading to $4,000 (was $300 when he started)
- Believes in eventual dollar devaluation + peg to gold/Bitcoin
- $37T national debt is unsolvable — some form of reset coming
- BRICS nations moving away from petrodollar system
- "Bitcoin will hit $1M someday"

### On Honesty
> "If people were honest with themselves, they'd know exactly why they're not making money."

- Most failing traders know the reason: external pressures, undercapitalization, lifestyle expenses
- They try shortcuts instead of doing the work
- "Look in the mirror. If you can't do that, you'll drain your account and blame everyone else."
